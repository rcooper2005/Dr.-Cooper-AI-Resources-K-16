🎯 Best Practices for AI in K-12 Education

✓ Start Small

Pick ONE tool that addresses your biggest challenge. Master it before adding more. Quality over quantity leads to sustainable integration.

✓ Always Review

AI makes mistakes. Always verify facts, check for bias, and edit outputs before sharing with students. Model critical evaluation.

✓ Be Transparent

Tell students how you're using AI. Model responsible use and help them develop AI literacy skills they'll need for their future.

✓ Age-Appropriate Tools

Match tools to developmental levels. Elementary students need structured, monitored experiences. Older students can handle more open-ended tools.

✓ Iterate & Improve

The best prompts come from practice. Save what works, refine over time, and share successful strategies with colleagues.

✓ Focus on Learning

AI should enhance—not replace—critical thinking. Use it to save time on routine tasks so you can focus on high-impact teaching.

🛡️ AI Safety Guidelines for K-16

Privacy & Data Protection

  • Never enter student names, IDs, or identifiable information into AI tools
  • Use school-approved tools with FERPA and COPPA compliance
  • Check your district's AI use policies before implementation
  • Obtain parent consent when required for student AI use
  • Keep student work and personal information separate from AI interactions

Appropriate Use by Age

  • Elementary (K-5): Teacher-mediated only; use tools like SchoolAI with guardrails
  • Middle School (6-8): Supervised use; focus on research tools with citations
  • High School (9-12): Guided independence; teach critical evaluation
  • College/University: Independent use with academic integrity policies; model ethical AI practices
  • Most general AI tools require users to be 13+ or 18+
  • Education-specific tools often have student-safe modes

Teaching AI Literacy

  • Explain that AI can make mistakes and produce biased content
  • Teach students to verify AI-generated information
  • Discuss ethical use and academic integrity
  • Model transparent AI use in your own work
  • Help students understand how AI works at an age-appropriate level
